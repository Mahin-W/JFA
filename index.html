01 — The Spark

Some people think of the Industrial Revolution as the most rapid advancement of technology in history, attributing modern inventions — the lightbulb, automobiles, trains — to those transformative decades. What if the growth of AI in just the last four years was more impactful than the entirety of that revolution?

02 — The Shift

My dad has been a software engineer for over 20 years. Whenever I'd look at his screen, I'd see thousands of lines of code. Last week, I visited him at work — and on his monitor, there wasn't a single line of code. Just a Claude Code chat. AI has completely overturned software and the internet. Five years ago, less than 5% of the internet was AI-generated. Today, over 50% is.

03 — The Origin

In 2026, most people can't imagine life without ChatGPT or Claude — but AI was first conceived in 1956 by John McCarthy, who coined the term "Artificial Intelligence" and imagined a machine that could reason and act on its own. From rule-based systems to machine learning to today's agentic AI, the evolution has been relentless. A perfect example: AutoGPT gained over 100,000 GitHub stars in a single day — one of the fastest-growing projects ever.

04 — My Story

My first real encounter with modern AI was in 2023 in 7th grade ELA, when a friend showed me ChatGPT. I asked it to write an essay on the Declaration of Independence and was stunned by the speed and quality. That feeling kept returning — when Claude Code and Codex made coding a single sentence, when Atlas, Cowork, and Openclaw let AI browse the web for you. People were buying Mac minis as AI-powered mini employees, paid a fraction of a human salary but exponentially more capable.

05 — The Future

The agentic AI sector is already valued at $7 billion — and in just nine years, it's projected to surpass $140 billion. Imagine companies no longer hiring employees, just paying for AI bots. Nobody ever has to learn a skill because AI knows how to do everything, and does it better than any human ever could. Combined with advanced robotics, AI may eventually make all jobs obsolete.
